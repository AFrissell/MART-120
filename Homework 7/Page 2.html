Short Story:

The Howling Man of The Bitteroot Mountains

In the great woods and mountains of Montana lives a howling man. From his back hangs two skis,

from his side hangs bear spray (for he is no foolish hiker).

He wanders the mountains, living amongst bears and other creatures.

Nobody knows the hills better than he. He's walked them a thousand times with leathered bare feet.

Every year hikers get lost in winter storms, out of cellular range and beyond the reach of roads.

The howling man of woods seeks them out and bring them to safety. His fur is thick, his beard is wild.

He has no need for coats, for his natural hair warms him enough, with thick tuffs of brown.

Entire ecosystems live in his brown mop. Things that crawl, slither, and fly.

He's seen the mountains shrink and grow. He speaks to trees and rocks and earns their wisdon with patience.

He feeds from berries and from fungus that grows beneath rocks. He lives to be alive.

When snow melts he walks his way into town and works as a cashier at the local gas station

in the early morning when only truckers and delivery men wander awake.

To the night owls,they hear only his howls late into the night.
